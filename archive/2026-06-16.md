# AI + STEM Education Daily Brief

## Key Developments
- UNESCO STEM Institute is actively seeking AI and education experts, with a deadline of June 19, 2026, to contribute to the field.
- Argonne National Laboratory is integrating AI research with education and training initiatives within STEM.
- The National Science Foundation (NSF) announced new funding opportunities to advance AI education and cultivate the future STEM workforce.
- F5 is providing STEM and AI Education Grants specifically targeting underserved communities to promote equitable access.
- Purdue University is launching professional development programs for Indiana educators, focusing on AI and STEM with LEGO® Education Computer Science & AI kits.
- Discussions are ongoing regarding "The Real Problem With AI in STEM Education" and the necessity for assessment reform in higher education due to generative AI use and misuse.
- Global evidence is being gathered on emerging technologies in STEM education concerning learning, equity, and their contribution to SDG4.

## Implications for Educators
- Educators with expertise in AI and education should consider opportunities to contribute to global initiatives, such as those by UNESCO.
- It is crucial for educators to engage with ongoing debates and challenges surrounding AI in STEM education to develop informed pedagogical approaches.
- Institutions and educators should actively seek out and apply for funding opportunities like those from the NSF to enhance AI and STEM curricula and workforce development.
- Educators in underserved communities should leverage grants, such as those from F5, to improve access to AI and STEM learning resources.
- Professional development incorporating hands-on tools and practical applications of AI, like the Purdue-LEGO initiative, offers valuable models for educator training.
- Higher education instructors must re-evaluate and reform assessment methods to effectively address the integration and responsible use of generative AI in learning.
- Educators should stay informed about research on emerging technologies in STEM, focusing on their impact on learning outcomes, equity, and broader societal goals.
